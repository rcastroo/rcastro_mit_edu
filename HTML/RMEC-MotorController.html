Project Overview
AutomataKit is a collection of open hardware and software being developed at the Center for Bits and Atoms (dir. Neil Gershenfeld) that provides rapidly reconfigurable sets of building blocks for the assembly of robotic systems and digital fabrication machinery. ATK is part of the CBA’s ongoing effort to share digital fabrication equipment and STEM education across the globe, and will be part of the ecosystem of tools used in a network of more than 1000 Fab Labs. A more detailed description of the project can be found here https://gitlab.cba.mit.edu/jakeread/automatakit One subset of ATK’s goals is to include motor control algorithms that adaptively learn their own tuning parameters during operation, to enable adaptable modular software to operate successfully with varying hardware. Among learning broadly how to design, develop and debug electrical circuits, firmware and hardware in a rapid prototyping context, the development of these algorithms are the main concentration of this project.
Personal Role & Responsabilities
The design goals coming from Broomba were set as follows: I will be responsible for the development of a modular, networked DC Motor Controller for robotic systems. This will include the development of an h-bridge circuit including current and position sensing hardware, as well as the firmware to control current and position loops. After hand-tuning control parameters for this controller, I will look to develop algorithms that search for the optimal set. Most importantly, I will test this adaptively tuned controller against a wide group of variable hardware. With time to spare, I will develop a personal CNC Machine using AutomataKit’s other networked control components. This is an opportunity for me to learn how to use the digital fabrication tools available to researchers in the CBA.
Circuit
This is my first attempt at a circuit board. It shall hold an H-bridge for motor control, current sensing, and SPI pins for encoder implementation. It is a board that revolves around the DRV8701 (http://www.ti.com/product/DRV8701) gate driver chip. The DRV8701 is capable of driving a varied set of MOSFETS. Chosen here are the AON7410 MOSFETS.
