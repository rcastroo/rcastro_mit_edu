Summary
This project is a design that I would love to make at some point over the next year. The "Magic Box" is used commonly at the MIT Edgerton Center to make the students we teach interested in science. The Magic Box is simply a cardboard box with a hole on one side. When you hit the box on the side, a "ring" of air flows out and you can make someone feel it from several meters away. It is a very fun activity, and this turret aims to automatize it. The end goal is to control this so that it automatically targets people's faces using a camera and a mechanism that automatically flows a ring of air out.

In order to keep the tradition of using cardboard as a critical material, most of the components of the turret are also made out of cardboard, which is why the gears are huuuuge and thicccc.
